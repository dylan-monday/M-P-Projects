You're paying for hosting, a separate SSL certificate, plugin maintenance, and security updates for infrastructure you don't need. It's like renting a warehouse to store a suitcase.

Two new sites. One shared engine. Built to sell.

### What LGM Gets

**A site that does the selling for you.**

The new LGM site leads with the data that makes cinema advertising undeniable. Every page is designed to move a prospect closer to picking up the phone — from animated stat callouts that hit harder than a PowerPoint deck, to a streamlined "get started" experience that signals affordability before anyone fills out a form.

**The Stats Engine.** An interactive, scroll-driven page that presents the cinema advertising story in a way no competitor is doing. The numbers animate, the comparisons are visual, and the whole thing is designed to be shared — by your sales team in emails, by prospects with their partners, by anyone making the case for cinema. This becomes a sales tool your team uses every day, not just a page on the site.

**In-Theater Lead Capture.** This is the one that gets me excited. LGM runs ads in theaters. So let's use that: a QR code in your own preshow ad that lets prospects scan, drop their info in 15 seconds (dark-mode, designed for a lit phone in a dark theater), and get a follow-up in their inbox an hour later — after the movie, when they're checking their phone in the car. It's not just a lead capture tool. It's a live proof of concept: "See how effective cinema advertising is? You just responded to one."

**Text-to-Info.** Same idea, different trigger. "Text BIGSCREEN to 55555." Auto-response delivers a beautifully designed Cinema Advertising Kit — stats, process, pricing signals, and a clear CTA. They text during the preshow, enjoy the movie, and have everything they need in their pocket by the time the credits roll.

**The Stats Kit.** A designed PDF that functions as your leave-behind, your email attachment, your follow-up closer. Every stat that matters, distilled into something a prospect can forward to a partner or co-owner. Not a brochure. A weapon.

**Smart Ad Upload.** For prospects who already have an ad, a frictionless upload path — drag-and-drop their file, enter their info, and they're in the pipeline. No back-and-forth email attachments. No "what format do you need?" The specs are right there, clearly written in plain English — not broadcast jargon. For prospects who *don't* have an ad, the page pivots seamlessly: "No ad? No problem. Here's what we'll build for you" — with creative examples from the PPP portfolio that show exactly what they'll get.

**Automated Follow-Up.** Every form submission, every QR scan, every text opt-in triggers a smart email sequence — not a newsletter blast, but a 3–4 touch cadence that nurtures without nagging. The right stat at the right moment. A testimonial that hits close to their industry. A soft CTA to schedule a call. Built once, runs forever. Your sales team wakes up to warm leads instead of cold forms.

**Objection Handling, Built In.** The site answers every question a prospect has before they ask it. How much does it cost? (Enough to signal affordability, not enough to anchor low.) What if I don't have an ad? (We make one — here are examples.) How fast can I get on screen? (48 hours.) How do I know it works? (Here are the numbers. Here's what other businesses say.) A comprehensive FAQ section handles the rest — and it's not buried on a subpage. It's woven throughout the experience, surfacing the right answer at the right moment.

### The Shared Ecosystem

Here's where the architecture gets interesting — and where the real leverage lives.

**Shared Content Library.** LGM and PPP operate in the same industry, serve overlapping audiences, and share the same proof points. Instead of maintaining two separate sets of testimonials, client logos, and stats, both sites pull from a single shared content library. A glowing testimonial from a PPP advertiser automatically becomes available to LGM. A new theater partner logo shows up on both sites. The cinema advertising stats live in one place and propagate everywhere. Update once, publish everywhere. This isn't just efficient — it means both brands compound each other's credibility.

**Simple Admin Dashboard.** You'll have a clean, minimal front-end — purpose-built, not a bloated CMS — where you can:

- **Add a new testimonial** — name, title, company, quote, logo upload. Assign it to LGM, PPP, or both. It's live within minutes.
- **Manage the client/partner logo library** — upload a logo, tag it (advertiser, theater, partner), and it appears in the right places across both sites.
- **Update key stats** — theater count, states served, renewal rate, advertiser count. Change a number once, it updates everywhere: both homepages, the stats page, the Stats Kit PDF, the email sequences.
- **Add creative examples** — upload a video thumbnail or clip to the PPP showcase. Tag it by industry. It's immediately browsable by prospects.
- **Control visibility** — this testimonial on PPP only, that one on both sites, this logo everywhere. Simple toggles.

No Webflow editor. No WordPress dashboard. No logging into anything complicated. Just the content you actually care about, managed simply, reflected instantly.

**Live Industry Data (Phase 2).** Both sites can pull in real-time signals from the cinema industry — top-performing films at the box office this week, major upcoming releases, industry trend data and news. Imagine:

- The LGM stats page showing not just "845 million projected moviegoers" but "This week's #1 film grossed $X million — audiences are showing up." Real-time relevance.
- The PPP theaters page featuring current box office performance alongside the partnership pitch — reinforcing that cinema is thriving and ad inventory is valuable.
- A "Cinema Industry Pulse" section that automatically surfaces relevant headlines — new box office records, audience trend data, major release announcements — keeping both sites fresh without anyone writing a blog post.

This kind of real-time context transforms a static website into something that gets *more* relevant every week. It also gives your sales team fresh talking points every time they open the site. "Did you see that [current blockbuster] just crossed $500M? That's your audience."
