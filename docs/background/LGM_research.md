## 1. Cinema Advertising: The Attention Advantage

Cinema is the highest-attention advertising medium available — by a wide margin.

### Attention Scores (NCM / Lumen / dentsu Study)

- Cinema attention scores are **4–7× greater** than TV, CTV, social, and digital ads across all brands and categories tested
- **97%** of moviegoers watch cinema ads vs. **38%** for TV and **35%** for CTV
- Viewers watch cinema ads **3× longer** than TV/CTV and **up to 10× longer** than social media ads
- Eye-tracking data shows cinema-goers watch **85% of an ad** without looking away — vs. **23%** on TV and **1.4%** online

_Sources: NCM/Lumen/dentsu US Cinema Attention Study (2023); Kinepolis/Lumen eye-tracking study_

### Recall & Memorization

- **76%** of moviegoers can recall ads seen before a film
- Cinema ads generate **2.5× more recall** than online video ads
- **1 cinema viewing** achieves the memorization that takes **5 TV viewings** or **17 poster exposures**
- Cinema ads deliver **30% more ROI** than outdoor billboards on average
- Cinema delivers **3× more brand lift** compared to TV in many markets

_Sources: Amra & Elma Cinema Marketing Statistics 2025; Kinepolis business research; AdzBasket 2025 report_

### The "No Skip Button" Factor

- **70%** of cinema ads are watched without distraction vs. **20%** for online ads
- Average mobile ad view time: **~2.2 seconds** (2025) — cinema delivers 15–30 seconds of full attention
- Direct correlation found between higher attention metrics and **increased sales lift, foot traffic, and app downloads**
- **61% of Gen Z** report posting or discussing cinema ads they find clever or emotional online

_Sources: NCM/Lumen study; AdzBasket; Amra & Elma_

## 3. Why Local Advertising Works

### Consumer Trust in Local

- **67%** of consumers trust local businesses more than online-only brands
- **55%** feel more emotionally connected to businesses in their area
- **63%** of people view brands in local advertising as part of their community
- **88%** of consumers say it's important to trust the businesses they transact with
- **72%** are willing to pay more for the quality service they believe local businesses offer

_Sources: Uberall consumer survey (4,000 respondents); Agility PR; PR Newswire; SCORE_

### Local Search & Action

- Nearly **50%** of all Google searches have local intent
- **76%+** of local searchers take action within a day
- **70%+** of shoppers who conduct a local search visit a store within 5 miles
- **80%** of consumers prefer ads that reference their local environment
- **Over 80%** of shoppers say they'd pay more to buy local
- **85% of Gen Z** shop locally at least a few times a month

_Sources: Amra & Elma Local Products Marketing Statistics 2025; US Chamber of Commerce; PYMNTS Intelligence_

### Small Business Marketing Landscape

- Average estimated small business ad budget: **$78,000/year** (2025)
- **53%** of business owners say standing out from competition is their #1 challenge
- **49%** cite budget constraints as a top challenge
- **25%** of US consumers use ad blockers online — cinema has no ad blockers
- Only **40%** of small businesses currently invest in search advertising
- Businesses with a marketing plan are **6.7× more likely** to report success
- **16%** of SMBs plan to invest less in traditional media — opportunity to reposition cinema as *premium*, not "traditional"

_Sources: Intuit 2025 Small Business Advertising Report; LocaliQ; SimpleTexting 2024_

### 8.2 Ad Upload & Self-Service Onboarding

**Reduce friction to zero for prospects who already have an ad.** Create a self-service path alongside the high-touch sales path.

- [ ] **"Upload Your Ad" flow** — A dedicated page or section on the Get Started page where a client with an existing ad can upload it directly. Should include:
  - File upload widget (drag-and-drop, accepts .mov, .mp4, .avi, .mxf — or link to Dropbox/Google Drive/WeTransfer)
  - Upload destination: whatever shared platform the client already uses (Google Drive shared folder, Dropbox Business, Frame.io, or a simple S3 bucket with pre-signed URLs)
  - Auto-confirmation email: "We got your ad. Here's what happens next."
  - Basic contact info capture alongside the upload (name, email, business, market)
- [ ] **Ad specs page** — Clear, jargon-light technical requirements for submitting a finished ad. Based on industry standards:

  **Video specs:**
  - Resolution: 1920×1080 (HD) minimum
  - Frame rate: 23.98/24fps (cinema standard) or 29.97/30fps (will be converted)
  - Codec: Apple ProRes, H.264 at maximum quality, or MPEG-4. Avoid heavily compressed web formats.
  - Aspect ratio: 16:9 (widescreen). Full frame — no letterboxing.
  - Duration: 15 or 30 seconds (most common). 60 seconds available in some programs.
  - Audio: 48kHz, 24-bit. Stereo minimum. 5.1 surround supported.
  - Keep all essential text/logos within the action-safe area (title-safe 90% of frame) — theater screen masking may crop edges.

  **Creative guidelines:**
  - Use bold, high-contrast colors — dark backgrounds with light text perform best (ambient theater light washes out light backgrounds)
  - Use thick, bold fonts — thin or script fonts don't read well at 50 feet
  - Avoid long panning shots — they create motion artifacts at 24fps
  - Include a clear call to action (phone, URL, QR code)
  - Content must be suitable for all ages (theaters have content approval policies)

  **Still image specs (for slide-based programs):**
  - Resolution: 1920×1080 or 2048×858 (DCI standard)
  - Format: JPEG, PNG, or TIFF — high resolution, no compression artifacts
  - Same creative guidelines as video (contrast, bold fonts, safe margins)

  **Don't have an ad?**
  - "No problem. Our in-house creative team has produced thousands of cinema-ready ads. We'll build one for you — it's included in many programs."
  - Link to creative showcase / sample reel

- [ ] **Brand asset collection form** — For clients who need an ad produced. A structured intake:
  - Upload your logo (vector preferred: .AI, .EPS, .SVG — raster accepted: .PNG, .PSD, .TIFF)
  - Upload photos (high-res product/location photos)
  - Key messaging: tagline, phone number, website, address
  - Any brand guidelines (colors, fonts, tone)
  - Textarea: "Tell us what you want people to know about your business"
  - This feeds directly to the PPP creative team

### 8.8 FAQ & Objection Handling

**Every question a prospect has that goes unanswered is a reason not to fill out the form.**

- [ ] **Comprehensive FAQ section** (accordion format, on /why-cinema or a dedicated page):
  - "How much does cinema advertising cost?" — Give the range. Don't hide it.
  - "What if I don't have an ad?" — We make one. It's included in many programs.
  - "How fast can I get on screen?" — 48 hours from approval.
  - "How long should my campaign run?" — Minimum 13 weeks recommended. Like any advertising, consistency drives results.
  - "Can I choose which movies my ad plays before?" — Your ad plays before every movie, every showing. Some networks allow genre/rating targeting.
  - "What theaters are available in my area?" — Link to /markets or "tell us your ZIP and we'll check."
  - "Can I target specific demographics?" — Yes — by theater location, market, and in some cases by film type.
  - "How do I know it's working?" — We'll review performance together. Many advertisers report increased calls, foot traffic, and brand mentions within weeks.
  - "What are the ad specs if I have my own?" — Link to ad specs page.
  - "Is there a minimum commitment?" — Typically 13 weeks. Ask us about trial programs.
  - "Can I change my ad during the campaign?" — Yes. Creative refreshes keep your message relevant.

### 8.10 Conversion Optimization & Analytics

- [ ] **Multi-path CTAs** — Not everyone converts the same way. Offer:
  - Form submission (primary)
  - Phone call (prominently displayed, click-to-call on mobile)
  - SMS ("Text BIGSCREEN to 55555")
  - Email (secondary)
  - Calendar booking link (Calendly or Cal.com for scheduling a 15-min call)

- [ ] **Form analytics** — Track form starts vs. completions (drop-off analysis). If people are starting the form and bailing, the form is too long or there's a friction point.

- [ ] **UTM-ready links** — All CTAs, emails, and SMS links should include UTM parameters so traffic sources are trackable in analytics. Critical for understanding which paths convert.

- [ ] **Heatmapping (optional)** — A lightweight tool like Microsoft Clarity (free) on the homepage and get-started page for the first 90 days to see where people scroll, click, and drop off.

- [ ] **Conversion events** — Set up proper event tracking for:
  - Form submissions (primary conversion)
  - Phone clicks (click-to-call)
  - PDF downloads
  - /go page visits (in-theater QR scans)
  - SMS opt-ins
  - Ad upload submissions
  - Email link clicks (from auto-responder sequence)
